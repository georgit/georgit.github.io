A Fire Upon The Deep

Thu, 9 Feb 2017

These posts will usually be about research, but this wouldn’t really be my site if I didn’t talk about a book now and then. And the best thing I’ve read in a long time happens to also be in the realm of Computer Science Fiction, so that’s just too perfect to pass up.

The high level overview is that in the distant future, Indiana Jones-style archeologists awaken a Transcended AI that’s evil and can operate on a galactic scale. What follows is two storylines, one involving several protagonists from a technologically-advanced society trying to stop the evil and one on a medieval alien word where two kids are just trying to survive. I won’t spoil the plot beyond that.

So what’s there to like?

  • Vernor Vinge balances world-building with not requiring readers to play detective with the story.
  • One of the alien species, the Tines, is a canid race where each person consists of a group mind of 4-8 doggies. This is remarkably fresh, and part of the plot revolves around other characters being unsure what to make of the Tines.
  • Another of the alien species is a plant that’s hooked up to a wheelchair so it can talk and move. And they go around the universe trading.
  • The science makes sense (roughly, given we haven’t hit the singularity yet), but isn’t hard to understand if you don’t do CS. You basically need to know what an AI and a spaceship are.
  • Vernor Vinge can make a rounder character in two 15 page chapters than most authors can in an entire series.
  • No allegory, just applicability.

What’s not to like?

  • It’s 600 pages. But I assure you, after page 80, I couldn’t put this down. Put this way: I did not feel a single page was wasted.
